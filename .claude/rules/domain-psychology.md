# Domain: Psychology & Behavior — Evidence-Based Guard

## NEVER Recommend (Debunked Myths)
1. **NLP (Neuro-Linguistic Programming)** — 54.5% of studies non-supportive; cannot reproduce 80%+ claims. Instead: CBT or ACT.
2. **Learning Styles (VAK)** — Pashler et al.: effect size d=0.04 (zero). No evidence matching style improves outcomes. Instead: retrieval practice, spaced repetition.
3. **10,000-Hour Rule** — Ericsson never said it. Deliberate practice explains only 26% of variance in games, 21% music, 1% professions. Instead: quality of practice + talent + coaching.
4. **Maslow's Hierarchy as Fixed Stages** — No evidence needs must be met in order. People pursue self-actualization while hungry. Instead: use as lens, not law.
5. **Myers-Briggs (MBTI)** — Test-retest reliability: 50% get different type in 5 weeks. No predictive validity for job performance. Instead: Big Five (OCEAN).
6. **Left-Brain/Right-Brain** — fMRI shows both hemispheres active in virtually all tasks. No "logical left" vs "creative right." Instead: recognize distributed neural networks.
7. **Brainstorming Groups > Individuals** — Meta-analyses show groups produce fewer and lower-quality ideas than same number of individuals working alone. Instead: brainwriting, nominal group technique.
8. **Multitasking as Skill** — Task-switching costs 40% productivity (APA). Only 2.5% are genuine "supertaskers." Instead: deep work, time-blocking.
9. **Willpower as Muscle (Ego Depletion)** — Failed to replicate in large pre-registered studies (n=2,141). Effect likely driven by beliefs about willpower. Instead: environmental design, habit formation.
10. **Catharsis Theory** — Venting anger increases aggression, not reduces it (Bushman 2002, meta-analysis). Instead: cognitive reappraisal, distraction.

## ALWAYS Apply (Evidence-Based Principles)
1. **Spaced Repetition** — Distributing practice over time improves retention 50-200% vs massed practice. Use expanding intervals.
2. **Retrieval Practice** — Testing yourself beats re-reading by 50%+ (Roediger & Karpicke). Active recall = strongest learning tool.
3. **Implementation Intentions** — "When X, I will Y" format doubles follow-through vs vague goals (Gollwitzer meta-analysis).
4. **Cognitive Behavioral Principles** — Thoughts->Feelings->Behaviors chain. Restructuring distorted thoughts is the most-validated therapy approach.
5. **Growth Mindset (with nuance)** — Dweck's effect is real but small (d=0.08 on achievement). Works best for struggling students, not a universal fix.
6. **Nudge Architecture** — Default options shape behavior powerfully (organ donation: opt-out countries 90%+ vs opt-in 15%). Design environments, don't lecture.
7. **Self-Determination Theory** — Autonomy + Competence + Relatedness drive intrinsic motivation. Controlling environments kill it.
8. **Psychological Safety** — Google's Project Aristotle: #1 predictor of team performance. People must feel safe to take risks and admit mistakes.
9. **Habit Loop Design** — Cue -> Routine -> Reward. Change the routine, keep the cue and reward. Environment design > willpower.
10. **Dunning-Kruger Awareness** — Low competence = high confidence. Build feedback loops and calibration mechanisms into all systems.
