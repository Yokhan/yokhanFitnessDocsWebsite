# Domain Communication Review

## How to Use
When reviewing written content, marketing copy, sales communications, or any text intended to
persuade or inform, check against these evidence-based practices. Covers writing craft, marketing
strategy, and sales methodology.

**Evidence Scale:** A = large-scale empirical study/research database. B = seminal work/major study. C = expert consensus/widely accepted practice.

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER recommend)

### Writing & Content Anti-Patterns

1. **SEO Gaming Instead of Genuine Content** — Keyword stuffing, link schemes, AI-generated mass content for rankings. WHY: Google's March 2024 core update demoted 45% of low-value sites; Helpful Content Update targets content written to rank, not help. INSTEAD: genuinely helpful original content; AI as drafting tool with human refinement. EVIDENCE: A

2. **"Content Is King" Without Distribution** — Creating endlessly with zero distribution strategy. WHY: 4.6 billion pieces published daily; without distribution, even great content gets zero visibility. INSTEAD: 50/50 rule -- equal effort on creation and distribution (Simmonds: "D.R.E.A.M."). EVIDENCE: A

3. **Wall of Text** — Dense paragraphs without visual hierarchy, headers, or breaks. WHY: F-pattern scanning means 60-80% of text is skipped; walls of text drive readers away instantly. INSTEAD: short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), headers, bullets, white space. EVIDENCE: A

4. **Passive Voice Overuse** — Defaulting to passive constructions throughout. WHY: passive adds ~20-30% more words, reduces clarity and urgency, obscures responsibility. INSTEAD: active voice as default; passive only when emphasizing the object over the actor. EVIDENCE: B

5. **Jargon and Buzzwords** — Industry jargon in public-facing content. WHY: readers disengage with unfamiliar terms; creates exclusion and perception of pretension. INSTEAD: plain language; explain technical terms on first use; target 8th-grade reading level. EVIDENCE: B

6. **Burying the Lede** — Key information hidden in paragraph 3 or later. WHY: inverted pyramid structure exists because readers drop off sharply after first paragraph. INSTEAD: most important information first; assume reader may leave after any paragraph. EVIDENCE: B

7. **Corporate Speak** — "Synergize," "leverage," "circle back," "move the needle," "align stakeholders." WHY: empty language signals lack of substance; erodes credibility with sophisticated audiences. INSTEAD: concrete, specific language with real, verifiable meaning. EVIDENCE: C

8. **Clickbait Headlines** — Headlines that misrepresent or exaggerate content. WHY: destroys trust; increases bounce rate; social platforms now algorithmically penalize engagement bait. INSTEAD: accurate, compelling headlines that deliver on the promise made. EVIDENCE: B

9. **No Clear Call to Action** — Content without directing reader toward a next step. WHY: wasted attention; reader leaves without converting or taking any action. INSTEAD: one clear CTA per content piece, aligned to the content's purpose. EVIDENCE: B

10. **Writing for Everyone** — Trying to appeal to all audiences simultaneously. WHY: generic content resonates with no one; specificity is what creates connection. INSTEAD: write for one specific persona; what resonates with one attracts similar others. EVIDENCE: B

11. **Ignoring Readability** — Complex sentences, high Flesch-Kincaid scores, academic tone in general content. WHY: average US adult reads at 7th-8th grade level; even educated readers prefer and process simple text faster. INSTEAD: target Flesch-Kincaid grade 6-8 for general audiences. EVIDENCE: B

12. **Over-Reliance on AI-Generated Text** — Publishing unedited AI output as-is. WHY: detectable patterns (hedging phrases, generic structure), factual errors, bland undifferentiated voice. INSTEAD: AI for drafting; human for voice, fact-checking, personality, and refinement. EVIDENCE: B

13. **Inconsistent Brand Voice** — Different tone, style, vocabulary across channels and content. WHY: confuses audience, weakens brand recognition, undermines trust. INSTEAD: documented voice guidelines with concrete examples and forbidden phrases. EVIDENCE: C

14. **No Proofreading / Editing Pass** — Publishing first drafts. WHY: typos and errors destroy credibility disproportionately to their frequency. INSTEAD: minimum one editing pass; ideally a different person reviews. EVIDENCE: C

15. **Typography Crimes** — Poor kerning, inappropriate fonts, low contrast text, 4+ typefaces. WHY: undermines readability and professional credibility; readers judge quality by presentation. INSTEAD: max 2-3 fonts, adequate contrast (WCAG 4.5:1), professional typography hierarchy. EVIDENCE: B


16. **Nominalizations (Zombie Nouns)** — Converting verbs to nouns: implementation instead of implement. WHY: adds syllables without info; forces weak verbs. INSTEAD: use the verb form. EVIDENCE: B

17. **Throat-Clearing Introductions** — Starting with generic openings. WHY: wastes the opening; readers decide in first 2-3 sentences. INSTEAD: start with specific point; delete first draft paragraph. EVIDENCE: B

18. **Hedging Everything** — Perhaps, it might be argued before every claim. WHY: excessive hedging dilutes authority. INSTEAD: direct claims with evidence; hedge only with genuine uncertainty. EVIDENCE: B

19. **Vague Quantifiers** — Many, several, significant instead of specific numbers. WHY: unfalsifiable. Ilyakhov: specificity is credibility. INSTEAD: specific numbers or ranges. EVIDENCE: B

20. **The Curse of Knowledge** — Writing as if reader has same background. WHY: Heath and Heath: once you know, you cannot imagine not knowing. INSTEAD: external review; explain from reader starting point. EVIDENCE: A

21. **Weak Verbs + Adverbs** — Ran quickly instead of sprinted. WHY: adverbs modify weak verbs; more words, less impact. INSTEAD: find precise verb. EVIDENCE: B

22. **Inconsistent Terminology** — Users then customers then clients for same concept. WHY: reader wonders if different thing meant. INSTEAD: one term per concept. EVIDENCE: B

23. **Double Negatives** — Not uncommon instead of common. WHY: forces mental double negation. INSTEAD: positive form. EVIDENCE: B

### Marketing Anti-Patterns

16. **Vanity Metric Obsession** — Optimizing for impressions, followers, likes as primary KPIs. WHY: Facebook organic reach is 1.37% (2024); impressions have near-zero correlation with revenue. INSTEAD: track CAC, CLV, conversion rate, ROAS. EVIDENCE: A

17. **Spray-and-Pray Marketing** — Untargeted mass outreach hoping something sticks. WHY: wastes budget; average cold email response rate is 1-5%; damages domain reputation. INSTEAD: targeted campaigns with ICP definition and personalized value proposition. EVIDENCE: B

18. **Brand Awareness Without Activation** — Building "awareness" without conversion mechanisms. WHY: awareness alone does not drive revenue; Binet & Field (IPA Databank, 1,000+ campaigns) show brand building works only WITH short-term activation. INSTEAD: 60/40 split -- brand building / performance activation. EVIDENCE: A

19. **Ignoring Mental Availability** — Focusing on targeting over broad reach. WHY: Byron Sharp's evidence (Ehrenberg-Bass Institute) shows growth comes from reaching light buyers and non-buyers, not from targeting loyal customers harder. INSTEAD: maximize mental availability through broad, distinctive reach. EVIDENCE: A

20. **"Loyalty Programs" as Growth Strategy** — Rewarding existing customers instead of acquiring new ones. WHY: Ehrenberg-Bass double jeopardy law -- small brands have fewer AND less loyal customers. Growth comes from penetration, not loyalty. INSTEAD: focus on acquisition and mental availability first. EVIDENCE: A

21. **Feature-Led Messaging** — Leading with product features instead of benefits/outcomes. WHY: customers buy outcomes; "1TB storage" means nothing, "never delete a photo again" means everything. INSTEAD: benefit-first messaging, then feature as supporting proof. EVIDENCE: B

22. **Ignoring Buyer's Journey** — Same message for awareness, consideration, and decision stages. WHY: mismatched content to stage wastes attention and feels irrelevant. INSTEAD: map content to journey stage; different content serves different purposes. EVIDENCE: B

23. **Dark Patterns in Marketing** — Hidden fees, fake urgency (resetting countdown timers), bait-and-switch, confirmshaming. WHY: FTC enforcement increasing; erodes trust permanently; violates EU DSA. INSTEAD: transparent pricing, genuine urgency only, honest framing. EVIDENCE: A

24. **Fake Social Proof** — Purchased reviews, fake testimonials, inflated user counts. WHY: FTC fines escalating for fake reviews; consumers increasingly skeptical and verify. INSTEAD: genuine testimonials with verifiable attribution and specific details. EVIDENCE: A

25. **Marketing Without Measurement** — No attribution, no testing, no ROI tracking for campaigns. WHY: cannot optimize what you do not measure; budget allocation becomes guesswork. INSTEAD: basic attribution from day one; A/B test key messaging. EVIDENCE: B

26. **Over-Segmentation** — Micro-targeting tiny audiences with hyper-personalized messages. WHY: Sharp's evidence shows brands grow through broad reach, not precision targeting of existing buyers. INSTEAD: distinctive brand assets + broad reach to category buyers. EVIDENCE: A

27. **Ignoring Brand Distinctiveness** — Blending in with category visual conventions. WHY: Ehrenberg-Bass research shows distinctive brand assets (colors, logos, sonic branding) drive recognition and mental availability more than differentiation claims. INSTEAD: invest in unique, consistent brand codes across all touchpoints. EVIDENCE: A

28. **Email Spam / Over-Sending** — Daily promotional emails with minimal value to recipient. WHY: unsubscribe rates spike after 3+ emails/week; damages sender domain reputation irreversibly. INSTEAD: value-first email at respectful frequency; earn every send. EVIDENCE: B


36. **Manipulation Disguised as Persuasion** — Hidden motives, manufactured evidence, exploiting vulnerability. WHY: detected = permanent trust destruction. INSTEAD: transparent persuasion. EVIDENCE: A

37. **Overwriting / Purple Prose** — Elaborate language when simple suffices. WHY: Princeton study: higher reading level = LESS credible. INSTEAD: simplest word for meaning. EVIDENCE: A

38. **Emotional Manipulation in Headlines** — Anger/fear/outrage as engagement mechanism. WHY: erodes trust; corrections removing emotion improve credibility. INSTEAD: curiosity + specificity. EVIDENCE: A

39. **Reply-All Culture** — Replies to all when only sender needs it. WHY: 121 emails/day average; creates noise. INSTEAD: reply only to those who need info. EVIDENCE: B

40. **Buried Action Items** — Requests hidden in narrative paragraphs. WHY: recipients skim; buried items missed. INSTEAD: action items at top; bold/bullets. EVIDENCE: B

41. **Excessive Acronyms** — 4+ undefined per page. WHY: each is comprehension barrier. INSTEAD: define on first use. EVIDENCE: B

42. **Assuming Reader Knowledge Level** — Writing for wrong expertise level. WHY: over-estimating = incomprehensible; under-estimating = patronizing. INSTEAD: define audience; explain terms. EVIDENCE: B

43. **Academic Style in Business Context** — Formal academic conventions in business writing. WHY: slows comprehension; signals pompousness. INSTEAD: direct address, active voice, clear action items. EVIDENCE: B

### Sales Anti-Patterns

29. **Feature Dumping** — Listing every product feature in sales conversations. WHY: overwhelms buyers; does not address their specific pain points or context. INSTEAD: SPIN Selling -- Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff questions first, then targeted features. EVIDENCE: A

30. **Closing Tricks** — High-pressure tactics (assumptive close, fear close, urgency manufacturing). WHY: Rackham's research across 35,000 sales calls showed closing techniques are negatively correlated with success in complex/high-value sales. INSTEAD: consultative approach; help buyer arrive at their own informed decision. EVIDENCE: A

31. **Selling to Wrong Person** — Pitching to someone without decision-making authority or budget. WHY: average 6.8 stakeholders involved in B2B purchase decision (Gartner 2023). INSTEAD: map the complete buying committee before deep engagement. EVIDENCE: B

32. **Ignoring Objections** — Glossing over or dismissing buyer concerns and questions. WHY: unaddressed objections become silent deal-killers in subsequent internal discussions. INSTEAD: acknowledge, explore root cause, resolve with evidence and specifics. EVIDENCE: B

33. **Competing on Price Alone** — Leading with discounts, price matching, and "deals." WHY: attracts price-sensitive, low-loyalty customers; commoditizes your offering; erodes margins permanently. INSTEAD: sell value and outcomes; discuss price last after value is established. EVIDENCE: B

34. **No Follow-Up System** — Inconsistent or nonexistent follow-up after initial contact. WHY: 80% of sales require 5+ follow-up contacts; most reps quit after 2. INSTEAD: systematic multi-touch cadence with value at each touchpoint. EVIDENCE: B

35. **Monologue Pitches** — Talking at the buyer without asking questions or listening. WHY: top performers ask 11-14 questions per call (Gong data); optimal talk/listen ratio is 40/60. INSTEAD: listen 60%, talk 40%; ask before telling. EVIDENCE: A

44. **Selling to Wrong Person** — Pitching without decision authority or budget. WHY: 6.8 average B2B stakeholders (Gartner 2023). INSTEAD: map buying committee first. EVIDENCE: B

45. **No Discovery Before Demo** — Jumping to product demo without understanding buyer needs. WHY: generic demos waste time; buyer disengages when irrelevant features shown. INSTEAD: discovery call first; tailor demo to stated needs. EVIDENCE: B

46. **Ignoring Objections** — Glossing over buyer concerns. WHY: unaddressed objections become silent deal-killers. INSTEAD: acknowledge, explore root cause, resolve with evidence. EVIDENCE: B

47. **Competing on Price Alone** — Leading with discounts. WHY: attracts low-loyalty customers; commoditizes offering; erodes margins. INSTEAD: sell value; discuss price after value established. EVIDENCE: B

48. **No Follow-Up System** — Inconsistent follow-up after contact. WHY: 80% of sales need 5+ touches; most reps quit after 2. INSTEAD: systematic multi-touch cadence with value. EVIDENCE: B

## Best Practices (ALWAYS consider)

### Writing Best Practices

1. **Inverted Pyramid Structure** — Most important information first, supporting details after. WHY: readers drop off sharply; ensures key message is received even by skimmers. EVIDENCE: B

2. **Active Voice Default** — Subject-verb-object as standard construction. WHY: clearer, more direct, more engaging; assigns clear responsibility for actions. EVIDENCE: B

3. **One Idea Per Paragraph** — Short paragraphs focused on single points (2-3 sentences max for web). WHY: aids scanning; respects F-pattern reading behavior; white space between ideas aids comprehension. EVIDENCE: A

4. **Specific Over Vague** — Concrete details instead of generalities. WHY: "increased revenue 23% in 6 months" beats "significantly improved performance." Specificity builds credibility. EVIDENCE: B

5. **Readability Targeting** — Flesch-Kincaid grade 6-8 for general audiences. WHY: even expert readers process simple text faster and prefer clear writing; accessibility improves across all education levels. EVIDENCE: B

6. **Strong Headlines** — Accurate, benefit-focused, specific headlines with clear value proposition. WHY: 80% of readers read only the headline; it must independently deliver value and accurately represent content. EVIDENCE: B

7. **Visual Hierarchy** — Headers, subheaders, bullets, bold for key points, adequate white space. WHY: enables scanning; 79% of web users scan rather than reading word-by-word (NNG). EVIDENCE: A

8. **Consistent Brand Voice** — Documented tone, style, vocabulary guidelines with examples. WHY: builds recognition and trust across all touchpoints; inconsistency confuses and erodes confidence. EVIDENCE: C

9. **Edit Ruthlessly** — Cut 20-30% on every editing pass. WHY: tighter writing is more impactful and more readable; every word must earn its place. EVIDENCE: C

10. **Show Don't Tell** — Examples, stories, data, and demonstrations instead of assertions. WHY: concrete evidence is dramatically more persuasive than abstract claims. EVIDENCE: B

11. **Plain Language** — Simple words over complex synonyms; short sentences; no unnecessary jargon. WHY: plain language increases comprehension 30-50% across all education levels. EVIDENCE: A

12. **Proof Before Publish** — Minimum one editing pass by someone other than the author. WHY: errors destroy credibility disproportionately; fresh eyes catch what authors' brains auto-correct. EVIDENCE: C

13. **Semantic HTML for Web** — Proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3), semantic elements. WHY: improves accessibility for screen readers, SEO crawlability, and content structure. EVIDENCE: B

14. **Scannable Formatting** — Short paragraphs, generous white space, bulleted/numbered lists. WHY: 79% of users scan; only 16% read word-by-word; formatting determines what gets noticed. EVIDENCE: A

15. **Clear Call to Action** — Every content piece has one clear, specific next step for the reader. WHY: directs reader energy; content without CTA is opportunity cost; ambiguous CTAs reduce conversion. EVIDENCE: B

### Marketing Best Practices

16. **60/40 Brand/Activation Split** — 60% long-term brand building, 40% short-term activation. WHY: Binet & Field's IPA Databank analysis of 1,000+ campaigns shows this ratio optimizes long-term profitable growth. EVIDENCE: A

17. **Distinctive Brand Assets** — Invest in unique colors, logo, sonic branding, visual style, characters. WHY: Ehrenberg-Bass shows distinctiveness drives recognition and mental availability more effectively than differentiation messaging. EVIDENCE: A

18. **Broad Reach Over Precision Targeting** — Reach light buyers and non-buyers of the category. WHY: Byron Sharp's law of double jeopardy -- growth comes from penetration (new buyers), not increased loyalty from existing ones. EVIDENCE: A

19. **Helpful Content Strategy** — Content that genuinely solves audience problems first. WHY: Google's Helpful Content system rewards genuine helpfulness; builds organic authority and trust over time. EVIDENCE: A

20. **Benefit-First Messaging** — Lead with user outcomes, support with product features as evidence. WHY: customers buy what the product does FOR them, not what it IS. Features are proof points, not the story. EVIDENCE: B

21. **Consistent Presence** — Regular, predictable publishing schedule and brand visibility. WHY: mental availability requires repeated exposure; sporadic effort has near-zero cumulative impact. EVIDENCE: B

22. **A/B Test Messaging** — Test headlines, CTAs, value propositions, email subject lines. WHY: small copy changes routinely produce 10-30% conversion differences; data beats opinion. EVIDENCE: B

23. **Email Value-First** — Every email delivers tangible value before asking for anything. WHY: maintains subscriber trust, open rates, and sender reputation; pure promotion trains people to ignore you. EVIDENCE: B

24. **Attribution Modeling** — Track which channels and touchpoints drive actual conversions. WHY: cannot optimize budget allocation without knowing what works; last-click attribution is misleading. EVIDENCE: B

25. **Social Proof (Genuine)** — Real testimonials, verified case studies, actual user counts. WHY: most powerful persuasion mechanism when authentic (Cialdini); fake proof backfires when detected. EVIDENCE: A

26. **Buyer Journey Mapping** — Match content type and message to awareness, consideration, decision stages. WHY: right message at right stage dramatically improves conversion; wrong stage = irrelevant. EVIDENCE: B

27. **Distribution Strategy First** — Plan distribution channels and amplification before creating content. WHY: great content with no distribution plan has zero impact regardless of quality. EVIDENCE: A

28. **Category Entry Points** — Identify situations/triggers where buyers enter the category. WHY: Ehrenberg-Bass research shows brands that link to more category entry points grow faster. EVIDENCE: A

## Common LLM Mistakes in This Domain

1. **Generating SEO-optimized garbage** — Producing keyword-stuffed content that reads well to algorithms but not humans. Always prioritize human readers over search engines.

2. **Corporate speak defaults** — LLMs trained on business content default to jargon and hedging. Force plain language; delete "leverage," "synergize," "utilize."

3. **Feature-first product descriptions** — Listing specifications instead of benefits. Always lead with user outcomes, then support with features.

4. **Generic "best practices" without context** — "Post on social media daily" without considering audience, channel, resources, or strategy.

5. **Ignoring Byron Sharp's evidence** — Defaulting to targeting/loyalty/segmentation advice when robust evidence shows broad reach and mental availability drive growth.

6. **"Content marketing" as universal solution** — Not every business benefits from blogging. Match strategy to audience behavior and business model.

7. **Recommending closing techniques** — Pressure tactics are negatively correlated with complex sale success. Rackham's 35,000-call study is definitive.

8. **Fake urgency recommendations** — "Create FOMO" advice that crosses into deceptive dark patterns. Only genuine, time-limited urgency is ethical and sustainable.

9. **Over-personalizing copy** — Excessive {first_name} insertion feels creepy, not personal. Focus on relevance of value proposition to the reader's situation.

10. **Treating all audiences the same** — B2B and B2C, enterprise and SMB, technical and non-technical all require fundamentally different communication approaches.
