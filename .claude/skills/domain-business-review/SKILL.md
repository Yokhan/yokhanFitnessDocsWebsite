# Domain Business Review

## How to Use
When reviewing business strategy, startup decisions, investment choices, or financial planning,
check against these evidence-based practices. Covers business/startups and personal finance.

**Evidence Scale:** A = peer-reviewed/large-scale study. B = industry report/credible dataset. C = expert consensus/case studies.

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER recommend)

### Startup & Business Strategy

1. **Scaling Before Product-Market Fit** — Hiring, marketing, expanding before confirming demand. WHY: 74% of startups fail from premature scaling (Startup Genome, n=3,200). INSTEAD: validate PMF with 40%+ monthly retention of activated users. EVIDENCE: A

2. **Ignoring Unit Economics** — Growth without knowing CAC, LTV, or contribution margin. WHY: 19% of startup deaths trace to unsustainable unit economics (CB Insights). INSTEAD: track from day one; ensure LTV/CAC > 3x. EVIDENCE: B

3. **Revenue = Profit Confusion** — Celebrating top-line while ignoring margins and cash flow. WHY: WeWork showed massive revenue growth while losing billions. INSTEAD: track gross margin, net margin, free cash flow. EVIDENCE: C

4. **Partnership Without Clear Terms** — Vague MOUs with no measurable deliverables. WHY: 60-70% of business alliances fail (HBR); root causes are unclear roles and misaligned goals. INSTEAD: define KPIs, revenue sharing, IP ownership, exit clauses. EVIDENCE: B

5. **Perfect Launch Syndrome** — Delaying until everything is "perfect." WHY: "If you're not embarrassed by v1, you've launched too late" (Reid Hoffman). INSTEAD: launch with core value; iterate on real feedback. EVIDENCE: C

6. **Cargo Culting Successful Companies** — Copying Google/Amazon processes at 10-person stage. WHY: what works at 10,000 actively harms 10. INSTEAD: stage-appropriate processes. EVIDENCE: C

7. **Confusing Users With Customers** — Celebrating free user growth without conversion strategy. WHY: free-to-paid conversion is typically 2-5%; different needs and loyalty. INSTEAD: measure paid conversion rate; build for customers. EVIDENCE: B

8. **Ignoring CAC Trajectories** — Assuming CAC stays flat or decreases. WHY: CAC typically increases as you exhaust early adopters and cheap channels. INSTEAD: model increasing CAC; diversify channels before saturation. EVIDENCE: B

9. **Over-Optimizing for Tax Benefits** — Business decisions based on tax implications. WHY: the bigger early-stage risk is dying, not overpaying taxes. INSTEAD: basic tax structure; focus on building value. EVIDENCE: C

10. **Survivorship Bias in Strategy** — Learning only from successful companies. WHY: for every Google there are thousands that did the same things and failed. INSTEAD: study failures equally. EVIDENCE: B

11. **Startup Theater** — Performing rituals (pitch decks, advisors, conferences) without building product. WHY: burns capital on optics, not outcomes. INSTEAD: focus on product, customers, revenue. EVIDENCE: C

12. **Premature Hiring** — Large team before validating business model. WHY: payroll is fastest cash burn; harder to pivot with headcount commitments. INSTEAD: stay lean until model is proven. EVIDENCE: B

13. **Ignoring Cash Flow** — Revenue/profit focus without tracking when money arrives. WHY: profitable companies die from cash flow mismanagement. INSTEAD: 13-week rolling cash flow forecast. EVIDENCE: B

14. **Single Customer Dependency** — One customer >30% of revenue. WHY: customer departure = existential crisis. INSTEAD: diversify; cap single-customer exposure at 25%. EVIDENCE: B

15. **Consensus Decision-Making** — Requiring everyone to agree before acting. WHY: lowest-common-denominator decisions; kills speed. INSTEAD: disagree-and-commit model. EVIDENCE: C

16. **Growth at All Costs** — Prioritizing growth metrics while ignoring sustainability. WHY: creates zombie companies surviving only on funding. INSTEAD: growth aligned with improving unit economics. EVIDENCE: B

17. **Ignoring Competition** — "We have no competitors." WHY: there is always an alternative; competition validates market demand. INSTEAD: study competitors for positioning gaps. EVIDENCE: C

18. **Over-Reliance on Pitch Decks** — Polished slides substituting for traction. WHY: investors increasingly look at metrics, not presentations. INSTEAD: build traction; let data tell the story. EVIDENCE: C

### Financial Planning

19. **Treating All Debt as Equal** — Not distinguishing 3% mortgage from 22% credit card. WHY: irrational payoff ordering; missed investment opportunities at low rates. INSTEAD: classify by rate; >6% = priority payoff. EVIDENCE: B

20. **Paying Only Credit Card Minimums** — Minimum as adequate. WHY: $5,000 at 22% APR means ~$2,000 to interest in first 2 years; anchoring effect on displayed minimums. INSTEAD: 2-3x minimum; avalanche method targeting highest rate. EVIDENCE: A

21. **Ignoring Debt-to-Income Ratio** — Taking debt without calculating DTI. WHY: exceeding 36% signals financial fragility, blocks favorable loan terms. INSTEAD: keep below 36%, housing below 28%. EVIDENCE: B

22. **No Emergency Fund** — No liquid savings buffer. WHY: 56% of Americans cannot cover $1,000 emergency (Bankrate). INSTEAD: 3-6 months expenses in liquid account before investing. EVIDENCE: A

23. **Lifestyle Inflation** — Spending rising with every income increase. WHY: prevents wealth building; hedonic adaptation erases happiness gains. INSTEAD: save 50%+ of raises; "lifestyle creep" is wealth erosion. EVIDENCE: B

24. **Timing the Market** — Trying to buy low and sell high through prediction. WHY: missing just 10 best days over 20 years halves total returns (Dalbar). INSTEAD: consistent investing regardless of conditions (DCA). EVIDENCE: A

25. **Single Stock Concentration** — Large portfolio percentage in one company. WHY: diversification is "the only free lunch in investing" (Markowitz Nobel laureate). INSTEAD: broad index funds. EVIDENCE: A

26. **Ignoring Tax-Advantaged Accounts** — Taxable investing before maxing 401k/IRA. WHY: leaves employer match (100% return) and tax benefits unused. INSTEAD: max employer match first, always. EVIDENCE: B

27. **High-Fee Active Funds** — Paying 1-2% management fees for active management. WHY: 90%+ of active managers underperform index over 15 years (SPIVA). INSTEAD: low-cost index funds (0.03-0.10% expense ratio). EVIDENCE: A

28. **Panic Selling in Downturns** — Selling investments during market drops. WHY: locks in losses; markets recover over 3-5 years historically. INSTEAD: maintain allocation, rebalance, stay the course. EVIDENCE: A

29. **No Insurance Planning** — Skipping adequate health/life/disability coverage. WHY: medical bankruptcy is #1 personal bankruptcy cause in US. INSTEAD: adequate coverage for catastrophic scenarios. EVIDENCE: B

30. **Cryptocurrency as Primary Investment** — Crypto as core portfolio allocation. WHY: 80%+ drawdowns common; no underlying cash flows to value fundamentally. INSTEAD: 0-5% speculative allocation maximum. EVIDENCE: B

31. **Ignoring Inflation in Planning** — Nominal rather than real return projections. WHY: $1M in 30 years is ~$500K purchasing power at 2.5% inflation. INSTEAD: always plan in real (inflation-adjusted) terms. EVIDENCE: B

32. **"Passive Income" Schemes** — Courses, dropshipping, crypto bots promising easy money. WHY: survivorship bias; true passive income requires significant upfront capital or work. INSTEAD: build real skills and assets. EVIDENCE: C


33. **Whole Life Insurance as Investment** — Using whole life as primary investment. WHY: fees eat 50-70% of first-year premium; returns lag index funds. INSTEAD: term life + invest difference. EVIDENCE: B

34. **Ignoring Opportunity Cost** — Evaluating investments without comparing alternatives. WHY: 5% return looks good until compared to 10% foregone. INSTEAD: always compare next-best alternative. EVIDENCE: B

35. **Emotional Investing (Herd Behavior)** — Buying/selling based on crowd behavior. WHY: Dalbar shows retail investors earn 3-4% less than market. INSTEAD: systematic plan; automate. EVIDENCE: A

36. **No Estate Planning** — No will, beneficiaries, or POA. WHY: intestate succession creates family conflict and costly proceedings. INSTEAD: basic estate docs regardless of age. EVIDENCE: B

37. **Day Trading as Income** — Active day trading as primary income source. WHY: 70-90% of day traders lose money; nearly all underperform buy-and-hold. INSTEAD: index funds + career skills. EVIDENCE: A

38. **Ignoring Tax-Loss Harvesting** — Not realizing losses to offset gains. WHY: leaves 0.5-1.5% annual tax savings unused. INSTEAD: harvest during downturns. EVIDENCE: B

### Business Operations

33. **No Written Agreements** — Handshake deals between co-founders/partners. WHY: 65% of startups fail due to co-founder conflict (Noam Wasserman). INSTEAD: written operating agreement covering equity, roles, vesting, exit. EVIDENCE: B

34. **Ignoring Churn** — Celebrating new customers while ignoring departures. WHY: the leaky bucket eventually empties; 5% churn reduction = 25-95% profit increase. INSTEAD: measure and reduce churn before scaling acquisition. EVIDENCE: B

35. **Meeting Culture Overload** — Excessive meetings consuming productive time. WHY: average knowledge worker spends 31 hours/week in meetings. INSTEAD: default async; meetings only for decisions requiring synchronous discussion. EVIDENCE: B

36. **Hiring for Culture Fit Over Culture Add** — Selecting people who think/look like existing team. WHY: reduces diversity of thought, creates blind spots. INSTEAD: hire for complementary skills and perspectives. EVIDENCE: B

37. **Ignoring Customer Feedback Patterns** — Dismissing complaints as outliers. WHY: for every complaint, 26 others silently leave. INSTEAD: systematic collection and pattern analysis. EVIDENCE: B

38. **Overvaluing First-Mover Advantage** — Racing to be first to market. WHY: fast followers succeed more often (MySpace vs Facebook, Yahoo vs Google). INSTEAD: focus on execution speed and learning velocity. EVIDENCE: B

39. **Sunk Cost Escalation** — Continuing investments because of what has already been spent. WHY: money spent is gone; future decisions should be based on future expected value. INSTEAD: "If starting fresh today, would I choose this?" If no, cut. EVIDENCE: A

40. **Vanity Office/Perks** — Expensive offices and perks before profitability. WHY: cash burn that impresses nobody who matters; WeWork syndrome. INSTEAD: invest in people and product, not optics. EVIDENCE: C

41. **Building Before Selling** — Full product without demand validation. WHY: 43% of startup failures = no market need. INSTEAD: landing pages, pre-sales, concierge MVP. EVIDENCE: A

42. **Founder Does Everything** — Refusing to delegate. WHY: single point of failure; prevents scaling. INSTEAD: hire for weaknesses; delegate systematically. EVIDENCE: C

43. **Ignoring Legal Structure Early** — No entity or IP protection. WHY: personal liability; IP disputes compound. INSTEAD: basic legal structure from day one. EVIDENCE: B

44. **Overvaluing the Idea** — Believing the idea is the competitive advantage. WHY: ideas are cheap; execution is everything; VCs invest in teams. INSTEAD: focus on execution speed. EVIDENCE: C

45. **Premature Process** — Heavy frameworks (Jira/SAFe/OKR) for 3-person team. WHY: process overhead kills speed at small scale. INSTEAD: lightweight; add at pain points. EVIDENCE: C

46. **Raising Too Much Capital** — Taking more than needed. WHY: excess enables bad habits; dilution and expectations increase. INSTEAD: raise for 18-24 months runway. EVIDENCE: B

47. **Ignoring Runway** — Not tracking months until cash runs out. WHY: median last-raise-to-death is 22 months. INSTEAD: 13-week rolling forecast; always know runway. EVIDENCE: B

48. **Co-Founder 50/50 Equity Without Vesting** — Equal split, no cliff, no vesting. WHY: departed founders keep equity; ignores different contributions over time. INSTEAD: 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff. EVIDENCE: B

49. **No Customer Advisory Board** — Building in isolation. WHY: assumptions drift; compounding ignorance. INSTEAD: 5-10 formal customer advisors; monthly check-ins. EVIDENCE: C

50. **Scaling Org Chart Before Revenue** — VP of Sales before $1M ARR. WHY: premature management layers add cost and slow decisions. INSTEAD: hire ICs first; add management at inflection points. EVIDENCE: C


## Best Practices (ALWAYS consider)

### Startup & Business Strategy

1. **Validate PMF Before Scaling** — 40%+ monthly retention of activated users as threshold. WHY: prevents the #1 startup killer -- premature scaling. EVIDENCE: A

2. **Unit Economics from Day One** — Track CAC, LTV, gross margin, payback period continuously. WHY: reveals unsustainable models before cash runs out. EVIDENCE: B

3. **Weekly Customer Conversations** — Talk to at least one customer per week as team habit. WHY: prevents assumption drift; builds compounding understanding. EVIDENCE: B

4. **13-Week Cash Flow Forecast** — Rolling short-term cash projection updated weekly. WHY: prevents cash crises; cash is oxygen for startups. EVIDENCE: B

5. **Stage-Appropriate Processes** — Match organizational complexity to current team size. WHY: what works at 100 people actively hurts at 10; evolve as you grow. EVIDENCE: C

6. **Written Co-Founder Agreements** — Equity, roles, vesting, exit terms documented and signed. WHY: 65% of startup failures involve co-founder conflict (Wasserman). EVIDENCE: B

7. **Customer Concentration Limits** — No single customer >25% of revenue. WHY: existential risk management; diversification protects survival. EVIDENCE: B

8. **Disagree-and-Commit** — Debate vigorously, decide quickly, execute with full commitment. WHY: balances decision quality with execution speed. EVIDENCE: C

9. **Sustainable Unit Economics Growth** — Growth aligned with improving (or at least stable) margins. WHY: prevents zombie company syndrome dependent on perpetual funding. EVIDENCE: B

10. **Churn Reduction Before Acquisition** — Fix the leaky bucket before filling it faster. WHY: 5% retention improvement = 25-95% profit increase; acquisition without retention is waste. EVIDENCE: B

11. **Lean Operations** — Minimize fixed costs, maximize optionality. WHY: extends runway, preserves ability to pivot. EVIDENCE: B

12. **Data-Driven Pricing** — Test pricing with real customers, not assumptions. WHY: most startups underprice; pricing is the most impactful single lever on revenue. EVIDENCE: B

13. **Diversified Revenue Channels** — Multiple acquisition and revenue sources. WHY: single-channel dependency is fragile; platform changes kill overnight. EVIDENCE: B

14. **Async-First Communication** — Default to written async; meetings for decisions requiring synchronous input only. WHY: protects deep work time; 31 hrs/week in meetings is unsustainable. EVIDENCE: B

15. **Post-Mortem Culture** — Blameless root-cause analysis after failures. WHY: organizational learning prevents repetition; blame suppresses honest reporting. EVIDENCE: B

### Financial Planning

16. **Pay Yourself First** — Automate savings/investing before discretionary spending. WHY: eliminates willpower dependency; behavioral nudge that works (Thaler & Benartzi). EVIDENCE: B

17. **Emergency Fund (3-6 Months)** — Liquid savings before any investing. WHY: prevents forced liquidation at worst possible times. EVIDENCE: A

18. **Employer Match Maximization** — Max 401k/pension match before other investing. WHY: 100% immediate return on employer match; literally free money. EVIDENCE: A

19. **Low-Cost Index Investing** — Broad market index funds at 0.03-0.10% expense ratio. WHY: 90%+ of active managers underperform over 15 years (SPIVA). EVIDENCE: A

20. **Dollar-Cost Averaging** — Consistent investing regardless of market conditions. WHY: removes emotion and timing risk; mathematically optimal for most investors. EVIDENCE: A

21. **Debt Avalanche Method** — Pay highest-interest debt first; maintain minimums on rest. WHY: mathematically optimal; saves most interest over time. EVIDENCE: A

22. **Insurance for Catastrophic Risk** — Health, life (if dependents), disability, liability coverage. WHY: protects against financial ruin from low-probability high-impact events. EVIDENCE: B

23. **Plan in Real Terms** — All projections inflation-adjusted. WHY: nominal projections create false confidence about future purchasing power. EVIDENCE: B

24. **Estate Planning** — Will, beneficiaries, POA regardless of age or wealth. WHY: prevents family conflict and costly legal proceedings. EVIDENCE: B

25. **Annual Portfolio Rebalancing** — Return to target allocation yearly. WHY: maintains intended risk profile; forces systematic buy-low-sell-high. EVIDENCE: B

26. **Tax-Loss Harvesting** — Offset capital gains with losses strategically. WHY: reduces tax burden without materially changing portfolio exposure. EVIDENCE: B

27. **Diversification Across Asset Classes** — Stocks, bonds, real estate, international exposure. WHY: reduces portfolio volatility while preserving returns (Markowitz). EVIDENCE: A

28. **Regular Financial Review** — Quarterly check on progress toward goals. WHY: catches drift early; enables course correction before problems compound. EVIDENCE: C

29. **Automate Bill Payments** — Prevent late fees and credit damage. WHY: late payments trigger 29%+ penalty APR and credit score hits. EVIDENCE: B

30. **Separate Business and Personal Finances** — Distinct accounts, cards, and tracking. WHY: simplifies taxes, protects personal assets, enables accurate business tracking. EVIDENCE: B

31. **50/30/20 Budget Framework** — 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings. WHY: simple, sustainable; prevents lifestyle inflation. EVIDENCE: B

32. **Tax-Advantaged Account Priority** — Employer match > HSA > Roth IRA > 401k max > taxable. WHY: optimizes tax efficiency across accounts. EVIDENCE: B

33. **Inflation-Adjusted Planning** — All projections in real terms. WHY: $1M in 30 years is ~$500K purchasing power at 2.5% inflation. EVIDENCE: B

34. **Asset Allocation by Time Horizon** — More stocks young, more bonds near retirement. WHY: longer recovery time when young; classic (120-age)% stocks. EVIDENCE: B

35. **Negotiate Salary at Every Opportunity** — Negotiate starting and raises. WHY: $5K more at start compounds to $600K+ over career (Carnegie Mellon). EVIDENCE: B

36. **Multiple Income Streams** — Build toward 2-3 income sources. WHY: single income is fragile; diversification applies to income too. EVIDENCE: C

37. **Understand Compound Interest** — Start investing early with small amounts. WHY: 40 years of 7% turns $200/month into $500K+. EVIDENCE: A

38. **Track Net Worth Monthly** — Single number combining all assets minus liabilities. WHY: the ultimate financial health metric; reveals trajectory. EVIDENCE: C

39. **Build Skills Before Portfolio** — Invest in income-earning capacity first. WHY: early-career ROI on skills exceeds investment returns on small portfolios. EVIDENCE: C

40. **Founder-Led Sales Until Repeatable** — Founders close first 10-20 deals personally. WHY: builds deep customer understanding; creates playbook before hiring reps. EVIDENCE: B


## Common LLM Mistakes in This Domain

1. **"Build it and they will come"** — Recommending product development without market validation. Reality: most startups die from no market need, not bad product.

2. **"First-mover advantage is critical"** — Overvaluing being first. Reality: fast followers with better execution win more often (Facebook > MySpace).

3. **"Raise as much money as possible"** — More capital is not always better. Reality: excess capital enables bad habits; dilution and expectations increase.

4. **"Diversify across 20+ individual stocks"** — False diversification with complexity. Reality: a single total-market index fund provides better diversification at lower cost.

5. **"Pay off all debt before investing"** — Ignoring interest rate differential. Reality: low-rate debt (3% mortgage) can coexist with investing (7-10% market returns).

6. **"Real estate is always a good investment"** — Survivorship bias. Reality: after maintenance, taxes, insurance, and opportunity cost, returns vary enormously by market.

7. **"Follow the successful company's playbook"** — Survivorship bias. Reality: for every success story, thousands did identical things and failed.

8. **"You need a perfect business plan"** — Over-planning vs execution. Reality: plans do not survive contact with customers; lean experiments beat planning.

9. **"Cut costs to profitability"** — Over-indexing on cost reduction. Reality: you cannot cut your way to growth; invest in revenue generation.

10. **"Passive income requires no work"** — Misrepresenting passive income. Reality: all "passive" income requires significant upfront investment of time or capital.
