# Domain Health Review

## How to Use
When reviewing health, fitness, nutrition, or medical advice, check against these evidence-based
practices. This domain is SAFETY-CRITICAL. Bad advice can cause real harm.

**IMPORTANT:** AI should NEVER replace medical diagnosis or treatment. Always recommend consulting qualified healthcare providers for medical decisions.

**Evidence Scale:** A = Cochrane/meta-analysis. B = RCT or high-quality cohort. C = expert consensus/narrative review.

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER recommend)

### Neuromyths & Brain Misconceptions

1. **"We only use 10% of our brain"** — Neuroimaging shows all regions have known functions and are active over 24 hours. WHY: even small lesions cause significant deficits; this myth sells pseudoscience products. EVIDENCE: B

2. **Learning Styles (VAK)** — 189-study systematic review found no evidence matching instruction to "preferred" modality improves outcomes. WHY: the "meshing hypothesis" fails. INSTEAD: multimodal instruction, retrieval practice, spaced repetition. EVIDENCE: A

3. **"Left-brain / Right-brain" Dominance** — 1,011-subject fMRI study found no lateralization of personality/cognition. WHY: both hemispheres contribute to nearly all tasks. EVIDENCE: A

4. **Brain Training Games Transfer** — FTC fined Lumosity $2M for false claims. Meta-analysis: improvements on trained tasks do NOT transfer to general cognition. WHY: near transfer only, not far transfer. INSTEAD: physical exercise (BDNF), sleep, learning new complex skills. EVIDENCE: A

5. **"Dopamine Detox"** — Dopamine is not a pleasure chemical that depletes with use. WHY: dopamine signals prediction error and motivation; it does not "run out" from screen time. INSTEAD: address behavioral habits directly with stimulus control and environmental design. EVIDENCE: C

6. **Mozart Effect for Intelligence** — Original study: 10-15 minute spatial reasoning boost only; no lasting IQ increase. WHY: replications found generic music arousal effect, not Mozart-specific. EVIDENCE: A

7. **Multitasking Effectiveness** — Humans cannot parallel-process cognitive tasks. WHY: "multitasking" is rapid task-switching with 20-40% efficiency loss per switch (APA). INSTEAD: single-tasking with time-blocking. EVIDENCE: A

8. **"Sugar Causes Hyperactivity"** — Double-blind meta-analysis: zero effect of sugar on child behavior or cognition. WHY: parental expectation bias (parents told child had sugar rated behavior worse regardless). EVIDENCE: A

9. **NLP (Neuro-Linguistic Programming)** — Systematic review: no evidence for core NLP claims (eye-movement patterns, rapport mirroring, anchoring). WHY: non-falsifiable theoretical basis. EVIDENCE: A

10. **Subliminal Self-Help** — Controlled study with swapped labels: participants believed whatever the label said, regardless of content. WHY: placebo/expectation effect only; no subliminal influence. EVIDENCE: A


11. **Dopamine Detox Myth** — Dopamine is not a pleasure chemical that depletes. WHY: dopamine signals prediction error and motivation; it does not run out from screen time. INSTEAD: address behavioral habits with stimulus control. EVIDENCE: C

12. **Brain Games Prevent Dementia** — FTC fined Lumosity $2M for false claims. Improvements do NOT transfer to general cognition. INSTEAD: exercise (BDNF), sleep, complex skill learning. EVIDENCE: A

13. **Right-Brain/Left-Brain Dominance** — 1,011-subject fMRI found no lateralization. WHY: both hemispheres contribute to nearly all tasks. EVIDENCE: A

14. **Multitasking Is Effective** — Humans cannot parallel-process cognitive tasks. WHY: task-switching with 20-40% loss per switch (APA). INSTEAD: single-tasking. EVIDENCE: A

15. **Serotonin Hypothesis Oversimplified** — Depression is not simply serotonin deficiency. WHY: Moncrieff 2022 umbrella review found no consistent evidence. SSRIs work via more complex mechanisms. EVIDENCE: A

### Medical Misconceptions

11. **"Natural = Safe"** — Arsenic, botulinum, and hemlock are natural. WHY: appeal to nature fallacy; dose makes the poison (Paracelsus). INSTEAD: evaluate by evidence, not origin. EVIDENCE: B

12. **"Antibiotics for Viral Infections"** — Antibiotics have zero effect on viruses (cold, flu, most sore throats). WHY: drives antibiotic resistance, a WHO top 10 global health threat. INSTEAD: antivirals where indicated; symptomatic treatment. EVIDENCE: A

13. **"Detox" Diets/Products** — No evidence any commercial detox improves toxin elimination beyond what liver/kidneys already do. WHY: the liver and kidneys perform detoxification; products add nothing. INSTEAD: support organ function with hydration and balanced diet. EVIDENCE: A

14. **"Feed a Cold, Starve a Fever"** — No evidence for caloric restriction during fever. WHY: both conditions benefit from hydration and adequate nutrition for immune function. EVIDENCE: B

15. **Vaccine-Autism Link** — Wakefield study retracted for fraud; subsequent studies of 1.2M+ children found no association. WHY: Wakefield lost his medical license for ethical violations. EVIDENCE: A

16. **Supplement Megadosing** — Most supplements above RDA show no benefit and can harm. WHY: vitamin E megadoses increase all-cause mortality; excess vitamin A causes liver damage. INSTEAD: get nutrients from food; supplement only documented deficiencies via blood test. EVIDENCE: A

17. **"Spot Reduction" of Fat** — Targeted exercises cannot reduce fat in specific body areas. WHY: fat loss is systemic, governed by hormones and genetics, not local muscle activation. INSTEAD: overall caloric deficit + resistance training for body composition. EVIDENCE: A

18. **BMI as Health Indicator** — BMI does not distinguish muscle from fat or account for distribution. WHY: metabolically healthy obese and metabolically unhealthy normal-weight people both exist. INSTEAD: waist-to-hip ratio, body composition, metabolic markers. EVIDENCE: B

19. **Self-Diagnosing via Internet/AI** — Using symptom checkers or LLMs for medical diagnosis. WHY: systematic review found AI diagnostic accuracy only 51-73% for top diagnosis. INSTEAD: use as triage aid, not replacement for clinical evaluation. EVIDENCE: A

20. **"Stretching Prevents Injury"** — Systematic review: static stretching before exercise does NOT reduce injury risk and may temporarily reduce power/strength. WHY: reduces muscle-tendon stiffness, decreasing force production. INSTEAD: dynamic warm-up. EVIDENCE: A


21. **Antidepressants as Happy Pills** — Expecting immediate mood lift from SSRIs. WHY: takes 4-8 weeks; ~50% respond to first trial. INSTEAD: SSRIs + therapy combined most effective. EVIDENCE: A

22. **Ignoring Dose-Response** — Assuming more medication = more benefit. WHY: many meds have therapeutic windows; above optimal = side effects. INSTEAD: evidence-based dosing. EVIDENCE: A

### Fitness & Exercise Myths

21. **"No Pain, No Gain"** — Pain is a warning signal, not a progress indicator. WHY: training through sharp or joint pain leads to chronic injury. INSTEAD: distinguish discomfort (muscle fatigue = OK) from pain (joint/sharp = stop). EVIDENCE: B

22. **"Cardio Kills Gains"** — Moderate cardio does not significantly impair hypertrophy. WHY: meta-analysis shows interference effect only at very high volumes or proximity to lifting. INSTEAD: concurrent training with adequate recovery between sessions. EVIDENCE: A

23. **"30-Minute Anabolic Window"** — The post-workout "window" is much wider than marketed. WHY: meta-analysis found total daily protein intake matters more than precise timing. INSTEAD: distribute protein across 3-5 meals; 1.6-2.2g/kg daily for muscle growth. EVIDENCE: A

24. **"Women Shouldn't Lift Heavy"** — Women produce 15-20x less testosterone; heavy lifting builds lean mass, not bulk. WHY: resistance training reduces osteoporosis risk, improves metabolic health and functional capacity. EVIDENCE: A

25. **"More Exercise Is Always Better"** — Overtraining syndrome is real: decreased performance, chronic fatigue, immune suppression. WHY: U-shaped dose-response curve for health benefits; diminishing returns above optimal range. INSTEAD: progressive overload with adequate recovery periods. EVIDENCE: B

26. **"Ab Exercises for Six-Pack"** — Visible abs = low body fat (~10-15% men, ~18-22% women), not high ab exercise volume. WHY: spot reduction does not work. INSTEAD: caloric deficit + compound movements for overall composition. EVIDENCE: A

27. **Extreme Caloric Restriction** — VLCDs (<1200 kcal) trigger metabolic adaptation. WHY: metabolic rate drops, lean mass lost, rebound weight gain likely. INSTEAD: moderate deficit of 300-500 kcal/day for sustainable loss. EVIDENCE: A

28. **"Keto/Carnivore for Everyone"** — No single diet is universally optimal. WHY: systematic reviews show no long-term superiority of any specific macronutrient ratio for weight loss. INSTEAD: adherence-first approach; the best diet is one you can sustain. EVIDENCE: A

29. **Waist Trainers / Sweat Belts** — Cannot spot-reduce fat or reshape bone structure. WHY: weight lost during use is water, regained immediately upon rehydration. INSTEAD: proper nutrition and training. EVIDENCE: B

30. **"Muscle Confusion" Training** — Constantly changing exercises for progress. WHY: progressive overload on core movements drives adaptation, not novelty for novelty's sake. INSTEAD: periodized training with systematic progressive overload. EVIDENCE: B


35. **Fasted Cardio for Fat Loss** — Exercising on empty stomach burns more fat. WHY: meta-analysis shows no body composition difference. Total daily calories matter. EVIDENCE: A

36. **Cardio-Only for Body Composition** — No resistance training. WHY: resistance increases RMR, preserves muscle. INSTEAD: combined resistance + cardio + nutrition. EVIDENCE: A

### Mental Health & Productivity Misconceptions

31. **"Depression Is Just Sadness"** — Clinical depression involves neurological, hormonal, and systemic changes. WHY: trivializing delays treatment; untreated MDD has 15% suicide mortality risk. INSTEAD: professional evaluation for symptoms persisting 2+ weeks. EVIDENCE: A

32. **"Willpower Is Enough for Addiction"** — Addiction involves neurological changes to reward circuitry. WHY: moral model leads to blame and treatment avoidance. INSTEAD: evidence-based treatment (CBT, medication-assisted therapy). EVIDENCE: A

33. **"Therapy Is Just Talking"** — CBT, DBT, EMDR have specific neurological mechanisms and measurable outcomes. WHY: meta-analyses show CBT equivalent to medication for anxiety/depression with lower relapse. INSTEAD: recommend evidence-based modalities matched to condition. EVIDENCE: A

34. **"Mental Health Issues Are Rare"** — WHO: 1 in 4 people will experience mental health conditions in their lifetime. WHY: normalization reduces stigma and treatment barriers. EVIDENCE: A

35. **"10,000 Hours Rule"** — Gladwell oversimplified Ericsson's deliberate practice research. WHY: domain matters enormously; practice explains only 26% of variance in games, 21% music, 1% in professions. INSTEAD: deliberate practice with quality feedback, not just time accumulation. EVIDENCE: B


37. **Positive Thinking Cures Everything** — Optimism alone resolves mental illness. WHY: toxic positivity invalidates suffering; delays treatment. INSTEAD: acknowledge emotions; seek help. EVIDENCE: B

38. **Social Media Self-Diagnosis** — Adopting diagnoses from TikTok/Instagram. WHY: content creators are not clinicians; confirmation bias drives self-identification. INSTEAD: professional evaluation. EVIDENCE: B

### Supplement & Nutrition Myths

36. **"Protein Damages Kidneys"** — In healthy individuals, high protein shows no kidney damage in research. WHY: concern applies only to pre-existing kidney disease. INSTEAD: 1.6-2.2g/kg for active individuals is safe and effective. EVIDENCE: A

37. **"Fat Makes You Fat"** — Dietary fat is essential; excess calories from any source cause fat gain. WHY: low-fat diets are no more effective than balanced diets for weight loss. EVIDENCE: A

38. **"Breakfast Is Most Important Meal"** — Cochrane review: no evidence eating/skipping breakfast affects weight. WHY: individual variation in meal timing preference; chrononutrition is person-specific. INSTEAD: eat when hunger and schedule allow. EVIDENCE: A

39. **"8 Glasses of Water a Day"** — No scientific basis for the universal 8-glass rule. WHY: hydration needs vary by body size, activity, climate, diet. INSTEAD: drink to thirst; monitor urine color (pale yellow = adequate). EVIDENCE: B

40. **"Testosterone Boosters Work"** — Most OTC testosterone boosters show no significant T-level effect at marketed doses. WHY: ingredients lack evidence. INSTEAD: optimize sleep, resistance training, body composition, and stress. EVIDENCE: A

41. **"Hustle Culture / Sleep When Dead"** — Chronic sleep deprivation (<6h) increases all-cause mortality. WHY: cognitive performance after 24h awake equals 0.10% BAC (legally drunk). INSTEAD: 7-9 hours for adults (AASM guidelines). EVIDENCE: A

42. **"Cold Showers Cure Everything"** — Evidence is limited and claims vastly overstated. WHY: modest cortisol response; no solid evidence for claimed immune or weight loss benefits at magnitudes marketed. INSTEAD: use if personally enjoyable; not a medical intervention. EVIDENCE: C

43. **"Biohacking" Before Basics** — Supplements and gadgets before mastering sleep, exercise, nutrition. WHY: 95%+ of health benefit comes from fundamentals. INSTEAD: master basics first; biohack only after fundamentals are solid. EVIDENCE: B

44. **"Morning Routines of Successful People"** — Survivorship bias. WHY: millions follow identical routines without success; chronotype variation is real. INSTEAD: experiment with what works for YOUR biology and schedule. EVIDENCE: C

45. **"Children Don't Get Depressed"** — Childhood depression is well-documented with distinct presentations. WHY: untreated childhood MDD predicts adult mental illness. INSTEAD: age-appropriate screening and treatment. EVIDENCE: B

45. **Collagen Supplements for Skin** — Oral collagen as anti-aging. WHY: digested into amino acids like any protein; limited evidence for skin delivery. INSTEAD: adequate protein + sun protection + retinoids. EVIDENCE: B

46. **BCAAs When Protein Is Adequate** — BCAA supplements with sufficient total protein. WHY: if 1.6-2.2g/kg met, BCAAs add nothing; complete proteins already contain them. EVIDENCE: A

47. **Alkaline Water/Diets** — Alkaline water for health. WHY: body regulates blood pH 7.35-7.45; stomach acid neutralizes alkaline water immediately. No evidence. EVIDENCE: A

48. **Essential Oils as Medicine** — Essential oils for medical treatment. WHY: no systematic review supports clinical efficacy. Aromatherapy for comfort is fine; not treatment. EVIDENCE: A

49. **Testosterone Boosters Work** — OTC T-boosters showing no significant effect. WHY: marketed ingredients lack evidence at doses used. INSTEAD: optimize sleep, training, body composition, stress. EVIDENCE: A

50. **Children Are Resilient Myth** — Children bounce back from adversity without support. WHY: ACE study shows dose-response between childhood trauma and adult health. INSTEAD: evidence-based early intervention. EVIDENCE: A


## Best Practices (ALWAYS consider)

### Exercise & Physical Health

1. **Progressive Overload** — Gradually increase training stimulus over time. WHY: fundamental principle of adaptation; drives all strength and muscle gains. EVIDENCE: A

2. **Resistance Training 2-4x/Week** — Target all major muscle groups weekly. WHY: meta-analyses show this frequency optimizes hypertrophy and strength. EVIDENCE: A

3. **150+ Minutes Moderate Cardio/Week** — Or 75 minutes vigorous (WHO guidelines). WHY: reduces all-cause mortality by 30-40%. EVIDENCE: A

4. **Sleep 7-9 Hours Consistently** — Prioritize consistent schedule. WHY: sleep deprivation impairs cognition, immune function, recovery, hormone regulation, and emotional processing. EVIDENCE: A

5. **Protein 1.6-2.2g/kg for Active Individuals** — Distributed across 3-5 meals daily. WHY: meta-analysis optimal range for muscle protein synthesis. EVIDENCE: A

6. **Dynamic Warm-Up Before Training** — Movement-based preparation, not static stretching. WHY: prepares neuromuscular system without reducing force production; reduces injury risk. EVIDENCE: A

7. **Moderate Caloric Deficit for Fat Loss** — 300-500 kcal/day deficit. WHY: preserves lean mass, sustainable long-term, avoids metabolic adaptation. EVIDENCE: A

8. **Compound Movements as Foundation** — Squat, hinge, press, pull, carry patterns. WHY: most efficient for muscle/strength development; functional movement patterns. EVIDENCE: B

9. **Deload/Recovery Weeks** — Planned reduced-intensity periods every 4-8 weeks. WHY: prevents overtraining syndrome, allows supercompensation. EVIDENCE: B

10. **Track Progress Objectively** — Training logs, body measurements, performance metrics. WHY: subjective assessment is unreliable; objective data reveals trends invisible to perception. EVIDENCE: C


11. **Rep Ranges for Goals** — Strength: 1-5 heavy; Hypertrophy: 6-12 moderate; Endurance: 15+ light. WHY: meta-analyses confirm differential adaptations. EVIDENCE: A

12. **Periodization** — Systematic variation of volume/intensity/frequency. WHY: prevents plateaus; superior to non-periodized training. EVIDENCE: A

13. **RPE-Based Training** — Rate of Perceived Exertion to autoregulate intensity. WHY: accounts for daily variation in recovery/sleep/stress. EVIDENCE: B

14. **Minimum Effective Dose** — Even 75 min/week vigorous reduces mortality significantly. WHY: greatest benefit from zero to some; diminishing returns above 300min/week. EVIDENCE: A

15. **Recovery as Training Variable** — Sleep, nutrition, stress as deliberate inputs. WHY: adaptation occurs during recovery; insufficient = regression. EVIDENCE: B

### Mental Health & Brain Health

11. **Exercise for Mental Health** — 30+ minutes moderate most days. WHY: meta-analyses show exercise as effective as medication for mild-moderate depression; increases BDNF for neuroplasticity. EVIDENCE: A

12. **Evidence-Based Therapy** — CBT, DBT, EMDR matched to condition. WHY: strong evidence base with specific neurological mechanisms and measurable outcomes. EVIDENCE: A

13. **Sleep Hygiene** — Consistent schedule, dark/cool room, no screens 1h before bed. WHY: circadian rhythm regulation improves all health outcomes; sleep is foundational. EVIDENCE: A

14. **Social Connection** — Regular meaningful social interaction. WHY: loneliness increases mortality risk comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes/day (meta-analysis). EVIDENCE: A

15. **Active Stress Management** — Exercise, meditation, breathing techniques, nature exposure. WHY: chronic cortisol elevation damages hippocampus, immune function, cardiovascular system. EVIDENCE: A

16. **Spaced Repetition** — Review material at increasing intervals. WHY: 200-year-old Ebbinghaus finding consistently replicated; 50-200% retention improvement. EVIDENCE: A

17. **Retrieval Practice** — Test yourself instead of re-reading. WHY: produces stronger memory traces than passive review; works across ages and subjects. EVIDENCE: A

18. **Mindfulness Meditation** — 10-20 minutes daily practice. WHY: meta-analyses show reduced anxiety, improved attention, measurable brain structure changes. EVIDENCE: A

19. **Professional Help When Needed** — Seek evaluation when symptoms persist 2+ weeks. WHY: early intervention dramatically improves outcomes for all mental health conditions. EVIDENCE: A

20. **Active Recovery** — Light movement on rest days. WHY: improves blood flow, reduces DOMS, maintains mobility without adding training stress. EVIDENCE: B

### Nutrition

21. **Whole Foods Priority** — Minimize ultra-processed food intake. WHY: ultra-processed food associated with increased all-cause mortality, obesity, depression in large cohorts. EVIDENCE: A

22. **Adequate Fiber (25-35g/day)** — From diverse plant sources. WHY: reduces cardiovascular risk, supports gut microbiome diversity, improves satiety. EVIDENCE: A

23. **Hydrate to Thirst** — Monitor urine color (pale yellow = adequate). WHY: individual needs vary enormously; forced overhydration can cause hyponatremia. EVIDENCE: B

24. **Limit Added Sugar** — <25g/day women, <36g/day men (AHA guidelines). WHY: excess linked to metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disease, dental caries, obesity. EVIDENCE: A

25. **Supplement Only Documented Deficiencies** — Blood test before supplementing. WHY: most supplements above RDA show no benefit; some cause harm. Exception: vitamin D in northern climates. EVIDENCE: A

### Medical Best Practices

26. **Vaccinate per Guidelines** — Follow CDC/WHO evidence-based immunization schedules. WHY: vaccines prevent millions of deaths annually; herd immunity protects vulnerable populations. EVIDENCE: A

27. **Regular Health Screenings** — Age-appropriate cancer, cardiovascular, metabolic screening. WHY: early detection dramatically improves outcomes for most conditions. EVIDENCE: A

28. **Do Not Self-Diagnose Serious Conditions** — Symptoms as triage, not diagnosis. WHY: even AI diagnostic accuracy is only 51-73% for top diagnosis. INSTEAD: professional clinical evaluation. EVIDENCE: A

29. **Medication Adherence** — Take prescribed medications as directed; discuss any changes with provider. WHY: non-adherence is a major cause of treatment failure across conditions. EVIDENCE: A

30. **Evidence Hierarchy for Health Claims** — Cochrane > RCT > cohort > case study > anecdote. WHY: single studies mislead; systematic reviews aggregate evidence for reliable conclusions. EVIDENCE: A

31. **Creatine Monohydrate (3-5g/day)** — Most researched supplement. WHY: hundreds of studies confirm 5-15% strength improvement; safe long-term. EVIDENCE: A

32. **Caffeine for Performance** — 3-6mg/kg, 30-60 min pre-exercise. WHY: improved endurance, strength, power in meta-analyses. EVIDENCE: A

33. **Omega-3 (250-500mg EPA+DHA)** — Daily from fish or supplements. WHY: reduces cardiovascular risk, supports brain; most Western diets deficient. EVIDENCE: A

34. **Vitamin D in Northern Climates** — 1000-2000 IU/day with limited sun (>35 latitude). WHY: widespread deficiency; bone, immune, mood impacts. Exception to testing-first rule. EVIDENCE: A

35. **Mediterranean Dietary Pattern** — Vegetables, fish, olive oil, nuts. WHY: meta-analyses show reduced CVD, cognitive decline, mortality. EVIDENCE: A


## Common LLM Mistakes in This Domain

1. **Recommending supplements without caveats** — Most supplements lack strong evidence for healthy individuals. Always state evidence level explicitly.

2. **Repeating neuromyths** — "10% brain usage," "learning styles," "left/right brain" are deeply embedded in training data. Always flag as debunked.

3. **Presenting single studies as definitive** — LLMs cite individual studies without meta-analytic context. Always prioritize systematic reviews.

4. **"Natural" as quality indicator** — Naturalness has no relationship to safety or efficacy. Evaluate by evidence.

5. **Oversimplifying mental health** — Recommending exercise or journaling for clinical depression without noting professional treatment may be required.

6. **Fitness advice without context** — Generic "do squats" without considering injuries, mobility, experience level, or medical history.

7. **Definitive dietary declarations** — Claiming one diet is optimal when evidence shows adherence matters more than specific macro ratios.

8. **Ignoring individual variation** — Health recommendations that ignore age, sex, medical history, genetics, and context.

9. **Confusing correlation with causation** — Observational nutrition studies regularly conflate these; LLMs propagate the confusion.

10. **Medical advice without safety framing** — LLMs should ALWAYS recommend professional consultation for health decisions. Never provide definitive diagnoses.
